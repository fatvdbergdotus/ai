# Diffusion models
Diffusion models are a powerful class of generative models in Machine Learning that create data by learning to reverse a gradual noising process. The core idea is to take real data (such as images), progressively add random noise to it over many steps until it becomes nearly pure noise, and then train a neural network to reverse this process step by step—transforming noise back into meaningful data. During generation, the model starts with random noise and iteratively refines it, removing noise in small increments until a coherent output (like a realistic image) emerges. This approach allows diffusion models to capture highly complex data distributions and produce high-quality, diverse outputs. For example, models like Stable Diffusion and DALL·E can generate detailed images from text prompts, while similar techniques are also being applied to audio, video, and 3D generation. Diffusion models are known for their stability during training and their ability to produce more diverse and less artifact-prone results compared to GANs, although they can be computationally intensive and slower during the generation process due to the many iterative steps involved.
