#  Generative Adversarial Network
A Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) is a powerful framework in Machine Learning designed for generating new, realistic data by training two neural networks in opposition to each other: a generator and a discriminator. The generator creates synthetic data (such as images) from random noise, while the discriminator evaluates whether the data is real (from the training set) or fake (produced by the generator). During training, both networks improve simultaneously—the generator learns to produce increasingly convincing outputs to fool the discriminator, while the discriminator becomes better at detecting fake data. This adversarial process continues until the generated data becomes highly realistic. For example, GANs are widely used to generate photorealistic human faces, enhance image resolution (super-resolution), and even create artwork or deepfake videos. Their ability to learn complex data distributions without explicit supervision makes them highly influential in modern AI, although they can be difficult to train due to issues like instability and mode collapse.
