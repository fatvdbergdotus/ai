# Visual Perception
Visual perception in Artificial Intelligence refers to the ability of machines to interpret and understand visual information from the world, such as images and videos, in a way that mimics human vision. It involves processing raw pixel data using techniques from computer vision and deep learning to identify objects, recognize patterns, detect motion, and extract meaningful features. For example, self-driving cars rely on visual perception to recognize traffic signs, pedestrians, and other vehicles in real time, enabling safe navigation on roads, while facial recognition systems can identify individuals by analyzing unique features in images. Similarly, applications like medical imaging use AI to detect diseases such as tumors in X-rays or MRIs, demonstrating how visual perception allows machines to transform visual data into actionable insights across various real-world domains.
