# Knowledge representation
Knowledge representation in Artificial Intelligence refers to the methods used to structure and organize information so that machines can understand, reason, and make decisions. It involves encoding real-world knowledge into formats such as logical rules, semantic networks, frames, or ontologies, enabling AI systems to process relationships between concepts and draw meaningful conclusions. For example, in logical representation, a system may use rules like “All humans are mortal” and “Socrates is human” to conclude that Socrates is mortal. In semantic networks, relationships are shown as connections, such as “Dog → is a → Animal.” Frames can represent structured data like a “Car” with attributes such as color and number of wheels, while rule-based systems use statements like “IF temperature > 38°C THEN fever.” These examples show how knowledge representation helps AI systems simulate intelligent behavior and solve real-world problems.
